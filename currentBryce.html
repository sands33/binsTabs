Bryce Binstadt obtained his MD and PhD (Immunology) degrees from Mayo Medical School and Mayo Graduate School in 1999. He served as a resident in Pediatrics at Children’s Hospital Boston and Boston Medical Center from 1999-2002, followed by pediatric rheumatology fellowship at Children’s Hospital Boston from 2002-2005. He was a postdoctoral fellow in the laboratory of Diane Mathis and Christophe Benoist at the Joslin Diabetes Center from 2003-2007. In 2007, he started his own laboratory in the University of Minnesota’s Center for Immunology. Bryce is a member of the Microbiology, Immunology, and Cancer Biology (MICaB) graduate faculty and a member of the Medical Scientist Training Program (MSTP) steering committee. He also directs the Pediatric Rheumatology Fellowship Program and sees patients in the Pediatric Rheumatology clinic at University of Minnesota Masonic Children’s Hospital. He enjoys waterskiing, tennis, and spending time with his family.
